# UK and European Standard Book Sizes Guide (v2)

This guide provides a comprehensive overview of the standard book dimensions used across the United Kingdom and Europe for both limp bound (paperback) and case bound (hardback) publishing.

## Standard Dimensions Table

| Format / Name | Dimensions (Height x Width) | Binding Type | Typical Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **A-Format** | 178 mm × 111 mm | Limp | Standard mass-market fiction paperbacks. |
| **B-Format** | 198 mm × 129 mm | Limp / Case | Most common size for UK fiction, literary fiction, and general non-fiction. |
| **Demy (Limp)** | 216 mm × 135 mm | Limp | Premium trade paperbacks, particularly literary fiction. |
| **Demy (Case Bound)** | 216 mm × 138 mm | Case | Standard hardback size for fiction and general non-fiction. |
| **American Royal** | 229 mm × 152 mm | Limp / Case | Standard US Trade size (6 × 9 inches); highly common for international distribution and print-on-demand (KDP/IngramSpark). |
| **Royal (Limp)** | 234 mm × 153 mm | Limp | Premium trade paperbacks, biographies, and airport/travel editions. |
| **Royal (Case Bound)** | 234 mm × 156 mm | Case | Standard size for commercial hardbacks and heavy non-fiction. |
| **Pinched Crown Quarto** | 248 mm × 171 mm | Case | A slimmer variation of the classic Quarto, often used for specialized non-fiction and textbooks. |
| **Crown Quarto** | 246 mm × 189 mm | Case | Shorter children's books, art books, photography, and poetry anthologies with wider aspect ratios. |
| **A5** | 210 mm × 148 mm | Limp / Case | Academic publishing, journals, and technical manuals. |
| **A4** | 297 mm × 210 mm | Limp / Case | Heavily illustrated art books, atlases, and children's picture books. |

## Important Technical Considerations

### 1. Page Trim vs. Overhang
* **Limp Bound (Paperback):** The dimensions specified above represent the final, exact trimmed page and cover size.
* **Case Bound (Hardback):** The internal text block pages will match these dimensions. However, the outer rigid boards (the case) typically extend an additional **3 mm to 4 mm** beyond the top, bottom, and fore-edge of the page block to provide protection.

### 2. Industry Metric Conversions
Many modern printing presses operate on imperial measurements inherited from traditional UK/US systems. The table below outlines how these common metric dimensions translate to standard trade terms:

* **American Royal:** Equivalent to the traditional US **6" × 9"** standard format.
* **B-Format:** Roughly corresponds to **5.1" × 7.8"**, the staple of British high-street bookshops.
* **Royal:** Roughly corresponds to **6.1" × 9.2"**, the baseline for major non-fiction hardcovers.
